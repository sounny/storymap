Task 2

Create a New Story

2 minutes
  1. Go to StoryMaps: storymaps.arcgis.com ‚Üí
  2. Sign in with your ESRI account
  3. Click "New story" button (big blue button)
  4. Select "Start from scratch"

Checkpoint: You should now see the StoryMap builder with a blank cover block ready for content.
Task 3

Design Your Cover

5 minutes

The cover is the first impression. Let's make it count!

  1. Click on the cover block to edit it
  2. Add a background:
    • Click "Add image or video"
    • Upload one of your photos (choose your best one!)
    • Or select "Browse" to use a stock image
  3. Add your title:
    • Click "Add title"
    • Type a compelling title (keep it short!)
    • Example: "Discovering Austin's Hidden Gems"
  4. Add a subtitle:
    • Click "Add subtitle"
    • Briefly describe your story
    • Example: "A local's guide to the best spots in Texas' capital"

üí° Pro Tips for Great Covers:

  • Use landscape-oriented images (wider than tall)
  • Avoid busy images that compete with text
  • Ensure good contrast between image and title
  • Keep titles under 8 words

üìù Try It:

Adjust the "focal point" of your cover image by clicking the focal point button. This controls which part of the image stays visible on different screen sizes.

Task 4

Add Your Introduction

5 minutes

After the cover, hook your readers with an engaging introduction.

  1. Click the "+" button below the cover
  2. Select "Text" from the menu
  3. Add a heading:
    • Type your section title
    • Highlight it and select "Heading 1" from the format menu
üìö
Chapters & Navigation: When you use Heading 1 (H1), it creates a new "chapter" in your story. If you enable Navigation in the Design panel, readers can jump directly to each H1 heading from a table of contents!
  1. Write your introduction:
    • 1-2 paragraphs explaining your place
    • Why does this place matter?
    • What will readers discover?

üìÑ Example Introduction:

Welcome to Zilker Park

Nestled in the heart of Austin, Texas, Zilker Park is more than just green space‚Äîit's the city's outdoor living room. From the natural spring-fed Barton Springs Pool to panoramic views of the downtown skyline, this 350-acre urban oasis offers something for everyone.

In this story, I'll take you on a journey through my favorite spots in this beloved park.

Task 5

Add an Express Map

8 minutes

Now let's add the geographic heart of your story‚Äîan interactive map!

  1. Click the "+" button to add a new block
  2. Select "Map" from the menu
  3. Choose "Express map"

    (This is the built-in map maker‚Äîperfect for quick maps)

  4. Search for your location:
    • Type your place name in the search bar
    • Press Enter or click the search result
    • The map will zoom to that location
  5. Add a point marker:
    • Click the "Add" button
    • Select "Add point"
    • Click on the map where you want the marker
    • Add a title and description in the popup
  6. Customize your marker:
    • Change the icon (pin, star, etc.)
    • Adjust the color
    • Add an image to the popup if desired
  7. Click "Place map" when finished
üí°
Express Maps vs Web Maps: Express Maps are created directly in StoryMaps‚Äîgreat for simple locator maps. For complex multi-layer maps with analysis, create a Web Map in ArcGIS Online first, then embed it.
üé®
Express Maps Drawing Tools: You can add more than just points! Try:
‚Ä¢ Numbered points (1, 2, 3...) for sequential tour stops
‚Ä¢ Lines for routes and paths
‚Ä¢ Freehand drawing for custom shapes
‚Ä¢ Arrows to show direction or highlight areas
‚Ä¢ Text annotations to label map features

üìù Stretch Goal:

Add 2-3 more point markers to highlight different features of your location. Connect them with a line if they form a route or path!

Task 6

Add More Content Blocks

5 minutes

Flesh out your story with additional content. Try adding at least two more blocks:

üì∏ Add an Image

  1. Click "+" ‚Üí "Image"
  2. Upload a photo
  3. Add a caption describing it
  4. Add alt text for accessibility

üí¨ Add a Quote

  1. Click "+" ‚Üí "Text"
  2. Type your quote
  3. Highlight and select "Quote" format
  4. Add attribution below

üîó Add a Button

  1. Click "+" ‚Üí "Button"
  2. Add button text (e.g., "Visit Official Website")
  3. Paste the URL
  4. Choose button style

üìù Suggested Structure:

  1. Cover (done)
  2. Introduction text (done)
  3. Map (done)
  4. Image with caption
  5. More descriptive text
  6. Another image or quote
  7. Closing text with call-to-action
Task 7

Apply a Theme

3 minutes

Give your story a polished look by applying a theme:

  1. Click the "Design" tab in the sidebar (paintbrush icon)
  2. Browse themes:
    • Summit (dark, professional)
    • Tidal (light, airy)
    • Mesa (warm, earthy)
    • Ridgeline (bold header line)
    • And more...
  3. Click a theme to preview it
  4. Optionally customize:
    • Adjust accent colors
    • Change font pairing
    • Modify cover style

üí° Theme Selection Tips:

  • Dark themes work well with bright/colorful images
  • Light themes are better for text-heavy stories
  • Match theme mood to your content (nature = earth tones)
Task 8

Preview and Publish

5 minutes

Time to see your story as readers will experience it!

  1. Click "Preview" in the top-right corner
  2. Test your story:
    • Scroll through the entire story
    • Click map markers and test interactivity
    • Check text for typos
  3. Test mobile view:
    • Click the phone icon in the preview toolbar
    • Make sure everything looks good on small screens
  4. Exit preview and make any needed fixes
  5. Click "Publish" when you're satisfied:
    • Choose "Everyone (public)" to share with the world
    • Or "Only me" if you want to keep it private for now
    • Click "Publish" to go live!
