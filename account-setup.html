Module 01

Creating Your ESRI Account

Get set up with a free ArcGIS Public Account to access StoryMaps and the ArcGIS ecosystem. This is your gateway to geographic storytelling.

Why This Matters: Geographic understanding requires tools that let us see, analyze, and communicate spatial relationships. By creating your account, you're gaining access to a platform that connects you to the world's largest geospatial community and data resources.

  • Create a free ESRI Public Account
  • Navigate to the StoryMaps platform and explore geographic resources
  • Discover how your account connects you to spatial data and collaboration opportunities

Step 2

Navigate to the Sign-Up Page

There are two ways to access StoryMaps:

üßá From ArcGIS Online

If you already have an account

Click the "waffle" menu (9-dot grid) in the top bar of your ArcGIS Online account, then select StoryMaps.

üîó Direct URL

Fastest way to get started

Go directly to storymaps.arcgis.com and sign in or create an account from there.

Let's create your free Public Account:

  1. Open your browser and go to: arcgis.com/home/signin.html ‚Üí
  2. Look for the "Create account" option at the bottom of the sign-in form.
Step 3

Select Account Type

You will be presented with a few options. It is critical that you choose the correct one for this workshop:

‚ö†Ô∏è
Avoid "Start a free trial": Do NOT select the trial option. It expires after 21 days and you may lose access to your work.
  1. Locate the "Create a public account" box (usually on the left side).
  2. Click the link: "Create an account ->" inside that box.
Step 4

Fill in Account Details

Enter your information to create the account.

üí°
Recommendation: Use a Personal Email (Gmail, Yahoo, iCloud, etc.).
Why? Your school or work email might already be associated with an ESRI organizational account (`.edu` or `.org`), which can cause conflicts during sign-up. specific URL prefixes. Unless you are sure about your organization's login procedure, a personal email is the safest route for this independent workshop.
  1. First & Last Name: Enter your real name.
  2. Email: Enter your personal email address.
  3. Confirm Email: Retype to ensure accuracy.
  4. Agreements: Review and check the terms of use.
  5. Click Next: Proceed to finalize your account.
Step 5

Verify Your Email

An activation link has been sent to your email address. It is currently waiting for you.

‚ö°
Critical Step: You MUST click the link in that email to finish the setup. Your account will not work until you do this.
  1. Go to your email inbox (check the "Promotions" or "Spam" folder if you don't see it).
  2. Open the email from "Esri".
  3. Click the activation link. This will confirm your account and log you in.
Step 6

Finalize Account Details

After clicking the activation link, you will be directed to a page to complete your profile. This is where you create your login credentials.

üë§
Username Tip: Choose a username that is professional and easy to remember. It will be part of your public profile URL.
  1. Username: Create a unique username (6-128 characters).
  2. Password: Create a strong password.
  3. Security Question: Select a question and answer for account recovery.
  4. Create Account: Click the button to finish the process.
Step 7

Access StoryMaps

Now that your account is set up, let's access the StoryMaps platform:

  1. Go directly to StoryMaps: storymaps.arcgis.com ‚Üí
  2. Click "Sign In" in the top right corner.

Method A: Personal Public Account (Recommended)

Use the credentials you just created.

üîê
Use "ArcGIS login" section (Top): Enter your username and password here.

Method B: Organizational Account (Advanced)

If you belong to a university or organization with an ArcGIS subscription, you can sign in using your organization's URL prefix. This grants access to paid features and unlimited storage.

üè¢
How to use:
1. Expand the "Your ArcGIS organization's URL" section.
2. Enter your organization's prefix (e.g., if your org URL is utexas.maps.arcgis.com, enter utexas).
3. Click "Continue" to be redirected to your institution's login page.

‚ùì Landed on a "Welcome" Screen?

Sometimes, especially the first time you log in, you might be taken to the general ArcGIS Online welcome page instead of the StoryMaps dashboard.

üëâ
Action Required: Scroll down to the "Make your first maps and apps" section and click the "Make a story" card.
üéâ
Congratulations! You now have access to StoryMaps. Whether using a public or organizational account, your dashboard is your home base for creating, managing, and sharing your stories.
