Getting Started

01

Create an ESRI Public Account

To use StoryMaps for free, you need an ArcGIS Public Account.

  1. Sign Up: Go to ArcGIS Sign In.
  2. Select: Choose "Create a Public Account".
  3. Details: Fill in your name and email. Verify your account via the email link.
  4. Log In: Once verified, you're ready to access the platform.
02

Access the Platform

Go to storymaps.arcgis.com and sign in with your new account.

Here you will see your dashboard where you can start new stories, view favorites, and manage your collections.

Platform Tour

The StoryBuilder Interface

The builder is divided into blocks. You stack blocks to build your story.

  • Cover: The first thing readers see. Make it impactful with a full-screen image or video.
  • Text: Add titles, paragraphs, and quotes.
  • Media: Upload images, videos, and audio directly.
  • Immersive: Use "Sidecar" or "Guided Tour" for scrolling map interactions.

Bringing in ArcGIS Assets

The Storytelling Process

Great stories aren't just written; they are designed. Follow this four-phase framework to craft compelling narratives.

01

Plan & Outline

Before you build, map it out.

  • Target Audience: Who are they? What do they already know?
  • Core Message: Define your "Rule of Three" (3 takeaways max).
  • Content Inventory: Gather your maps, images, and videos.
  • Storyboard: Draft your narrative arc on paper first.
02

Build & Visualize

Assemble your story blocks.

  • Immersive Blocks: Use 'Sidecar' for scrolling map narratives.
  • Express Maps: Create simple locator maps directly in the builder.
  • Media: Ensure images are high-res (1920px+) but optimized.
  • Map Action: Link text to map movements for dynamic storytelling.
03

Refine & Polish

Details make the design.

  • Visual Hierarchy: Use headers (H1, H2) to guide the reader.
  • Theme: Choose a color palette that matches your mood.
  • Accessibility: Add Alt-text to all images and maps.
  • Review: Check your story on mobile devices.
04

Publish & Share

Release it to the world.

  • Metadata: Edit title, summary, and thumbnail for social sharing.
  • Visibility: Set permissions (Private, Organization, or Public).
  • Embed: Integrate your story into other portfolio sites.
